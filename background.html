In Session:

Riley  v. California

Yuvraj Lakhotia

The Facts

Name: Riley v. California

Case 573 U.S. 373

Granted: January 27, 2014

Argued: April 29, 2014

Decided: June 25, 2014

August 2, 2009: David Leon Riley and members of the Lincoln Park Bloods open fire on a rival gang member driving by, subsequently escaping in Riley’s red Oldsmobile.

August 22, 2009: Riley is pulled over for driving on expired license registration tags, driving a different car. Due to his license suspension, the car was impounded and the officers performed a routine inventory search of the vehicle. During this, they discovered two guns concealed in socks and hidden under the engine hood. Riley was arrested for the possession of concealed and loaded firearms.

Riley’s cell phone was taken from his pocket and analyzed by a gang unit detective. They concluded Riley’s gang affiliation with the Bloods after finding images of him performing gang signs along with other indicia from his phone.

Ballistics tests tied Riley to the August 2 shooting, finally charging him with shooting at an occupied vehicle, attempted murder, and assault with a semi-automatic firearm. Riley tried suppressing the evidence discovered on his phone, claiming that it violated his rights given by the Fourth Amendment, since no warrant had been issued prior to the search of his cell phone.

Following gang experts’ testimony, Riley’s affiliation was cemented and the motives behind the August 2 shooting were exposed. Riley was convicted of all charges and sentenced fifteen years to life in prison, affirmed by the California Court of Appeal, Fourth District, Division 1.
