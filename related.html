Riley  v. California

Yuvraj Lakhotia

Related Cases



Chimel v. California (1969)

  • Searches only limited to area within immediate control of suspect
  • Need warrant to search the rest of the house, belongings, etc.
  • Set the principle that warrantless searches are only for safety of officer and preservation of evidence

United States v. Robinson (1973)

  • Officers searched through crumpled cigarette pack and found vials of heroin
  • Cited as an exception to principles set in Chimel v. California because the "officer was entitled to inspect it" upon discovery
  • However, the court declined to extend Robinson to searches of data on cell phones

United States v. Wurie (2014)

  • Decided together with Riley v. California
  • Officers searched Wurie's call logs, discovering where he stored more drugs, firearms, and ammunition
  • Applying the same principles as Riley, the court ruled in favor of Wurie and required a warrant to search through call logs of an arrested individual
