In Session:

Riley  v. California

Yuvraj Lakhotia

Constitutionality


Amendments Evaluated: IV

"The right of the people to be secure in their persons, houses, papers, and effects, against unreasonable searches and seizures, shall not be violated, and no Warrants shall issue, but upon probable cause, supported by Oath or affirmation, and particularly describing the place to be searched, and the persons or things to be seized."

The case had important implications on the scope of the Fourth Amendment's protections against unreasonable searches and seizures.


Constitutional Questions:

Was the evidence admitted at trial from Riley's cell phone discovered through a search that violated his Fourth Amendment right to be free from unreasonable searches?
