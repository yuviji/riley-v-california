In Session:

Riley  v. California

Yuvraj Lakhotia

Analysis

Explain how the court decision expanded or limited an individual’s constitutional rights

The decision expanded an individual’s constitutional rights since they were now safe from the warrantless search of their cell phones after arrest. The court unanimously held that these searches were unconstitutional and found to be in violation of the individual’s Fourth Amendment rights. The court declared that cell phones were considered more than just a simple belonging, such as a pen, due to their “immense storage capacity” and the “privacies of life” they contain.



Explain how the court decision expanded or limited the power of the state

The decision limited the power of the state by restricting what law enforcement could and could not analyze during a warrantless search. Due to the decision, police officers performing an arrest are now required to request a warrant from a judge in order to scan the contents of an individual’s cell phone. They are, however, allowed to physically inspect it, in case it can be used as a weapon to harm an officer or to check if it contains hidden items.
